I am a Ph.D. candidate under Bertrand TOËN and Lukas BRANTNER, based at the Institut de Mathématiques de Toulouse, Université Paul Sabatier. Previously, I obtained my bachelor degree at Wuhan University, and my master degree in Toulouse with a scholarship from CIMI.

My research interests lie in derived algebraic geometry, particularly in derived foliations and Lie algebroids in general characteristics. I mainly use the tools of higher categories and homotopical algebra, while analytic methods also attract my attention.

Research

  • A duality between Lie algebroids and infinitesimal foliations. arxiv

    Partition Lie algebroids and infinitesimal derived foliations are two derived analogues algebraic foliations in general characteristics. We establish a divided power Koszul duality between them under some finiteness conditions.

  • 2024 Octorber at Angers: Formal geometry of algebraic foliations. note

    The holomorphic Frobenius theorem states that every smooth holomorphic foliation is locally a fibration. However, its analogue in algebraic geometry fails manifoldly. This problem motivates infinitesimal derived foliations (in the sense of Toën and Vezzosi). In this talk, we will show that they are essentially equivalent to partition Lie algebroids, and discuss the application to the formal geometry of foliations over general bases.
